Care Guide.

Handmade things reward attentive care. These are not rules — they are the habits of someone who loves what they own.

General care

🫧

Washing

Hand wash in cool water with a mild, pH-neutral soap. Turn inside out. Do not wring — press gently between towels to remove water.

☀️

Drying

Lay flat or hang in the shade to dry. Avoid direct sunlight for dyed pieces — natural colours will deepen beautifully with light, but prolonged sun exposure accelerates the process unevenly.

🌡️

Ironing

Iron on a low to medium heat, inside out. Steam is preferred. Many of our pieces are intended to carry gentle wrinkle — it is part of the cloth's character.

📦

Storage

Fold loosely and store away from direct light. Avoid plastic bags — natural fibres need to breathe. Cedar blocks rather than mothballs for wool and silk blends.

Natural dyes

Our colours are drawn from plants, minerals, and bark — indigo, turmeric, iron, tannin, and others. This means:

  • Slight colour variation between pieces is natural and intentional.
  • Colours deepen and mellow with age rather than fading uniformly.
  • The first few washes may release excess dye — this is normal. Wash separately in cool water initially.
  • Avoid soaking for extended periods, which can draw colour unevenly.
"A naturally dyed garment is still in conversation with the world. It changes because you wear it, because you wash it, because time moves through it. We consider this the cloth's biography, not its failure."

Fabric-specific notes

Handloomed cotton & kantha: Grows softer with each wash. Machine wash on delicate cold if hand washing is impractical — but hand washing extends the life of the stitching.

Jamdani & muslin: Very delicate. Hand wash only, cool water, minimal agitation. These are among the finest woven fabrics in the world — treat them accordingly.

Endi silk blends: Dry clean recommended, or hand wash in cool water with silk-specific soap. Do not wring.

Belgian linen: Machine wash cool, lay flat to dry. Linen softens significantly with washing — a stiff new linen piece is simply one that hasn't been loved yet.
