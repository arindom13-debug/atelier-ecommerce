The Maker's Mark

Four promises, kept by hand.

Hand-loomed

Every thread placed by a single weaver's hands.

Naturally dyed

Colour drawn from roots, bark, and indigo leaves.

Small batches

Never more than forty pieces cut per pattern.

Mended for life

We repair every piece, free, without question.

In the Atelier

Where the work begins.

Before a garment is ever worn, it is made — slowly, by hand, in rooms that smell of cotton and indigo. Every seam carries the unhurried attention of the person who set it.

See the Process →
Our Craft

Made slowly.
Made once.

Each piece begins at the loom. It takes one of our weavers three full days to produce enough fabric for a single garment — a pace we have never tried to accelerate. At Atelier, slowness is not a constraint. It is the work itself, made visible in every thread.

Read Our Story → Meet the makers →
Heritage Est. Dhaka — 1975

Fifty years of quiet making.

Our founders arrived in Dhaka with a single belief: that enduring things demand time. Since 1975, we have worked with the same families of weavers, passing techniques not through manuals, but through hands. The knowledge lives in the fabric. You carry it with you.
